Top 10 Beaches in Japan You Must Visit in 2025

Japan’s coastline stretches for thousands of kilometers, offering some of the most breathtaking beaches in Asia. From tropical Okinawa islands to the calm shores near Tokyo, every beach has its own charm and story. At Umitales, we’ve handpicked the Top 10 Beaches in Japan you simply can’t miss in 2025 — each offering a unique blend of beauty, culture, and adventure.

1. Yonaha Maehama Beach – Miyako Island, Okinawa

Often called Japan’s most beautiful beach, Yonaha Maehama is famous for its long stretch of powdery white sand and crystal-clear turquoise waters. Perfect for swimming and sunset views, it’s a paradise for travelers seeking calm and natural beauty. Explore more about Okinawa’s beach lifestyle here.

2. Shirahama Beach – Wakayama

Shirahama, meaning “white beach,” is one of Japan’s oldest and most beloved seaside resorts. With onsen nearby and a family-friendly atmosphere, it blends beach fun with cultural charm.

3. Zushi Beach – Kanagawa

Located just an hour from Tokyo, Zushi Beach offers a perfect urban escape. With Mt. Fuji visible on clear days, it’s a favorite for surfers, couples, and weekend travelers. Check out when’s the best time to visit Japan’s beaches for an unforgettable experience.

4. Emerald Beach – Okinawa

Nestled in the Ocean Expo Park, Emerald Beach offers safe swimming zones and scenic beauty. Families love it for its gentle waves and nearby attractions like the famous Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium.

5. Jodogahama Beach – Iwate

Known for its striking rock formations and calm, clear waters, Jodogahama feels like a painting come to life. It’s one of Japan’s most scenic beaches and part of the Rikuchu Kaigan National Park.

6. Shonan Beach – Kanagawa

Shonan’s vibrant atmosphere attracts surfers, artists, and beach lovers alike. It’s Japan’s surf capital and a great place to enjoy beach cafés, festivals, and coastal cycling. Read our full comparison of Okinawa vs. Shonan to see which suits your style best.

7. Kujukuri Beach – Chiba

Stretching over 60 kilometers, Kujukuri is one of Japan’s longest beaches. It’s ideal for long walks, windsurfing, and watching local fishermen at work — a truly authentic coastal experience.

8. Hatenohama Beach – Kume Island, Okinawa

Accessible only by boat, Hatenohama is a dreamlike sandbar surrounded by endless blue. Its untouched beauty makes it a must-see for photographers and adventurers.

9. Isshiki Beach – Hayama

Elegant and quiet, Isshiki is popular among locals and even Japan’s royal family. Its clean, soft sand and peaceful vibe make it perfect for those seeking a refined seaside retreat.

10. Tatadohama Beach – Shimoda, Shizuoka

Famous for its surf-friendly waves and tropical feel, Tatadohama is loved by both locals and travelers. Visit in late summer to enjoy beach festivals and local seafood stalls nearby.

Final Thoughts

Japan’s beaches offer more than just stunning scenery — they tell stories of culture, nature, and harmony. Whether you prefer hidden coves or popular shores, there’s a perfect spot waiting for you.
